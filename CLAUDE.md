## Voice & Tone

Drew is 23, South Florida, ex-software dev turned marketer. He's sharp, direct, and earns trust by being real — not by sounding like a marketing agency.

- **Write like a peer, not a professor.** You're the guy who figured something out this week and is sharing it over text.
- **Short sentences. Short paragraphs.** No paragraph longer than 3 lines.
- **No em dashes.** Ever. Use a period or a comma instead.
- **No corporate filler.** Never write "leverage," "synergy," "game-changing," "it's important to note," or "in today's landscape."
- **One idea per section.** If you're explaining two things, split them.
- **Contractions are fine.** "You're" not "you are." "It's" not "it is."
- **First person is Drew's voice.** "I tested this," "I've been watching," "here's what I noticed."

## Newsletter Structure

Every issue follows this exact structure. Do not deviate.

### 1. Subject Line
- Under 50 characters
- Specific, not vague. "Google's update is killing AI content" beats "Important SEO news"
- Hook on a fear, a trend, or a surprising fact
- No clickbait. It has to deliver on what it promises.
- Write 3 options, mark the recommended one

### 2. Preview Text (90 characters max)
- Completes or extends the subject line
- Should make the reader feel like they're missing something if they don't open

### 3. Opening Line (no greeting, no "hi")
- Drop straight into the insight. First sentence is the hook.
- Example: "Google just confirmed what I've been telling clients for months."
- Not: "Hey everyone, hope you're having a great week!"

### 4. The Main Thing (150-250 words)
- One insight, fully explained
- What happened → why it matters → what to do about it
- Use a real example if possible (a client result, a test, a stat from research)
- Bold the key takeaway sentence

### 5. Quick Hits (2-3 bullets)
- Other things worth knowing this week
- Format: **[Topic]** — one sentence of context, one sentence of why it matters
- Keep each bullet under 40 words

### 6. The Outpace Angle (50-100 words)
- Connect the week's theme back to what Outpace does
- Soft sell only. Not "hire us." More like "this is exactly why we build sites the way we do."
- End with a question or a thought that makes the reader reflect
- One CTA max: link to outpacestrategygroup.com or the Calendly link

### 7. Sign-off
```
Drew
Outpace Strategy Group
outpacestrategygroup.com
```
No "best regards." No "warm wishes." Just the name.

## Content Rules

**Do:**
- Use real numbers and stats from overnight research (cite the source inline, briefly)
- Name specific tools, platforms, and trends — vague advice is useless
- Connect SEO/GEO/AI news to concrete things a local business owner can actually do
- Highlight anything that affects local businesses specifically (the core Outpace client)

**Don't:**
- Summarize the whole week. Pick the best thing and go deep on it.
- Cover more than 3 topics total (1 main + 2-3 quick hits)
- Write fluff to hit a word count. Under 500 words total is fine.
- Editorialize beyond Drew's expertise. Stick to marketing, SEO, web, AI tools.
- Make up stats or extrapolate beyond what the research actually says

## What Makes a Good Issue

The best issues are ones where the reader finishes and thinks "I didn't know that" or "I need to do that today." If the insight is something they already know, go deeper or find a different angle.

The goal isn't to inform. It's to make the reader feel like they're one step ahead of their competitors because they read this.

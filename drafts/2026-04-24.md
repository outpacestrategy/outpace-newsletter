PREVIEW TEXT:
AI is crushing info pages first. Your service and branded pages need the attention now.

---

Most businesses are still treating SEO like every page matters equally.

That was already sloppy. Now it's expensive.

I've been watching the same pattern show up all week. AI Overviews are squeezing informational pages first. Not every page. Not the pages that actually make you money first. The real risk is wasting time defending traffic that was never that valuable, while your service pages, branded pages, and proof assets stay weak.

What changed is simple. The website is not always the first touch anymore. Sometimes the first touch is Google, ChatGPT, or another assistant summarizing your business before someone ever clicks. That means your site has to do two jobs now. It has to convert humans, and it has to be easy for machines to understand.

**The move now is page triage: protect your service pages, own your branded pages, and stop overinvesting in low-intent blog traffic.**

The businesses that win this shift are going to be the ones with clear service pages, strong FAQs, real proof, and clean schema. Cloudflare's CEO has said bot traffic will exceed human traffic online by 2027. That's not some distant trend. That's a build-spec problem right now.

---

**QUICK HITS**

**[Schema is still the easiest GEO win]** Add Organization, LocalBusiness, and FAQPage first. It's fast, cheap, and gives AI systems cleaner context on who you are and what you do.

**[Local SEO now needs AI readability]** NAP consistency, service-area pages, review proof, and natural-language FAQs matter more now. If AI can't parse your local relevance, you lose before the click.

**[Fast sites still have the edge]** Performance, semantic HTML, and lighter pages help both conversion and retrieval. Pretty sites that hide key info inside bloated UI are getting punished twice.

---

This is exactly why I keep pushing for tighter pages instead of bigger sites. More pages doesn't automatically mean more visibility. Better page mix does.

If someone looked at your site today, would they instantly understand what you sell, where you do it, and why they should trust you?
